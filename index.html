Jana Nenadalová

I am a scholar specializing in the application of predictive processing frameworks to the study of religious experience. My work bridges empirical, lab-based research with anthropological insights into how rituals and religious phenomena unfold in naturalistic settings. I am especially interested in how cognitive models can illuminate embodied religious practices and the ways in which cultural environments shape experiential dynamics. My aim is to connect experimental rigor with deep contextual sensitivity to better understand religious expression in all its complexity.
